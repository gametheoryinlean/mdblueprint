## Generality Gate

Strong complementarity: the most general useful admitted form appears to be the LP-backed matrix-game strong-complementarity theorem. The math node carries the proof-oriented route through `node:math.linear_programming.strong_complementarity`, while the zero-sum node is a domain-facing consequence. It is unclear whether the narrower zero-sum-facing formulation is deliberate enough to keep as a separate node, so refactor-005 is semantic-review, not mechanical-safe.

Fair division: the most general allocation and instance abstractions already exist at `node:social_choice.fair_division.allocation`, `node:social_choice.fair_division.cardinal_instance`, and `node:social_choice.fair_division.share_instance`. The divisible and indivisible variants are deliberate specializations, not automatic duplicates. Refactor-004 adds missing specialization ancestry instead of requesting new nodes or merging variants.

LP minimax bridge: the most useful form is not a merge of the player LP nodes. The row-player and column-player LP formulations are dual but distinct, and `node:math.linear_programming.minimax_bridge.zero_sum_lp_bridge` already records the bridge role.

Extensive-game staged overlaps: the with-chance and imperfect-information staged nodes are broader or formulation-specific variants of admitted no-chance or perfect-information nodes. Because their ids already exist in the staged index, this report writes no missing-node request and makes no promotion proposal.

Preference topic ownership: the most general Lean interface lives in `EconCSLib.Foundation.Preference`, while admitted wrapper nodes currently live under social choice. Whether to move primary ownership to foundation or keep social-choice ownership with secondary topic membership is a human taxonomy decision.

The math node has LP strong-complementarity support, while the zero-sum node gives the game-theory-facing statement. Affected status: blocked on semantic equivalence and canonical-owner review.

For refactor-009 through refactor-011, no body or dependency edit is proposed yet. The formulation-impact risk is high because the target nodes have many descendants and cross-topic uses. Descendant validity must be reviewed by statement role and by alternate ancestors, not by graph reachability alone.

The dry-run plan validates structural effects only. It does not validate mathematical truth, proof correctness, or semantic alignment.
